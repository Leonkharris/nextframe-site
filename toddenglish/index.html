The Legacy

A Culinary Legacy Spanning Three Decades

William Todd English is one of America's most celebrated chefs, restaurateurs, and hospitality visionaries. A four-time James Beard Award winner and member of the James Beard Foundation's "Who's Who in Food & Beverage in America," he has spent more than three decades creating award-winning restaurant concepts, luxury hotel dining programs, and food halls.

A graduate with honors from the Culinary Institute of America (1982), English honed his skills in New York under Jean-Jacques Rachou at the legendary La Côte Basque, before spending years in Italy apprenticing at renowned kitchens in Milan, Florence, and Bologna. In 1989, English opened his flagship restaurant, Olives, in Charlestown, MA. It became an immediate national sensation, drawing praise for its woodfired oven flavors.

Today, as the co-founder of English Hospitality Group (EHG) alongside Keith Burkard, English continues to lead global operations encompassing upscale restaurants, hotels, casino concessions, airport pubs, and luxury residential amenities worldwide.

Chef Todd English in the kitchen
Flagship Culinary Art

bluezoo

Walt Disney World Dolphin · Orlando, FL

Designed by world-renowned architect Jeffrey Beers, bluezoo transforms coastal cuisine into fine art. Located inside the Walt Disney World Dolphin Resort in Orlando, FL, bluezoo represents Chef English's flagship seafood concept. The venue features a spectacular custom wood-fired grill, raw bar, and underwater-inspired design.

Chef English combines the freshest seafood with coastal culinary techniques from across the globe, creating signature creations such as the citrus-glazed pan-seared red snapper. This award-winning venue has served as a Walt Disney World dining anchor for over two decades.

Jade by Todd English

Berawa · Canggu, Bali

Set in the heart of Bali's vibrant Berawa district in Canggu, Jade merges rich Mediterranean flavors with exotic Southeast Asian spices. The dining sanctuary features lush tropical landscaping, rich velvet lounges, custom wood carvings, and an elegant cocktail bar.

Designed as a transition venue, guests enjoy refined shared plates like wood-grilled satays, lobster handrolls, and wagyu dumplings before the space shifts into a high-energy cocktail lounge as the night unfolds.

The Beast

AREA15 · Las Vegas, NV

Situated inside AREA15, Las Vegas's pioneering immersive entertainment complex, The Beast is an industrial-steampunk gastropub. Spanning over 15,000 square feet, the multi-concept food hall format features oversized copper wheels, steel machinery, and theatrical open-flame kitchens.

Serving as the primary culinary anchor for AREA15, The Beast offers an indulgent menu of artisanal woodfired pizzas, house-smoked ribs, double-patty sliders, and theatrical signature drinks, delivering dining that is as much an attraction as it is a restaurant.

Tuscany by Todd English

Mohegan Sun · Uncasville, CT

Tuscany is Mohegan Sun's premier 250-seat regional Italian restaurant, celebrating Todd English's culinary roots. Following a comprehensive multi-million dollar renovation in early 2026, Tuscany features an expanded modern bar, a wood-fired grill hearth, and a dining room overlooking the casino's indoor waterfall.

The menu showcases artisanal house-made pastas, dry-aged steaks, and flatbreads cooked over fruitwood embers, offering regional Italian dishes crafted with a modern, upscale presentation.

The Olives Heritage

The story of Todd English's global brand began in Charlestown, Massachusetts, with the opening of Olives in 1989. Designed as a casual, high-energy neighborhood bistro, Olives introduced Americans to bold Mediterranean flavors, woodfired baking, and rustic presentation. The restaurant's massive success led to extensions at the Bellagio in Las Vegas, Atlantis Resort in the Bahamas, Tokyo, and Abu Dhabi, defining upscale dining for an entire generation.

Television & Media

A Pioneer of Culinary Television

Long before the modern era of social media, Todd English was one of the first young chefs to glamorize the culinary world on television. As the host and executive producer of the Emmy-nominated PBS series "Food Trip with Todd English", he traveled the world tracing the origins of signature ingredients. He served as co-host and judge of PBS's "Cooking Under Fire", starred on Food Network's "Opening Soon", and has been a frequent guest chef on Iron Chef, Top Chef, the Today Show, Good Morning America, and Martha Stewart, building a household name with millions of viewers.
